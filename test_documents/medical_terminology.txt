Medical Terminology Guide

Basic Medical Terms:
1. Diagnosis - The identification of the nature of an illness or other problem
2. Prognosis - A forecast of the probable outcome of a disease
3. Symptom - A physical or mental feature indicating a condition or disease
4. Syndrome - A group of symptoms that consistently occur together
5. Acute - Having a rapid onset, severe symptoms, and a short course
6. Chronic - Persisting for a long time or constantly recurring

Common Medical Abbreviations:
- BP: Blood Pressure
- HR: Heart Rate
- CBC: Complete Blood Count
- MRI: Magnetic Resonance Imaging
- CT: Computed Tomography
- ECG/EKG: Electrocardiogram

Body Systems:
- Cardiovascular System: Heart and blood vessels
- Respiratory System: Lungs and airways
- Digestive System: Stomach, intestines, and related organs
- Nervous System: Brain, spinal cord, and nerves
- Musculoskeletal System: Bones, muscles, and joints

Medical Specialties:
- Cardiology: Study of the heart
- Dermatology: Study of the skin
- Neurology: Study of the nervous system
- Orthopedics: Study of bones and joints
- Pediatrics: Medical care of children
